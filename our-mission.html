Our Mission

Empowering African’s, Orphans, Widows and families.

The Aids pandemic is just not affecting adults perhaps even more cruelly, it is affecting children and Africa has been hardest hit 90% of the world’s orphans live in sub-Sahara Africa – were Zambia falls. Millions of children in sub-Sahara Africa have lost or both parents due to Aids. UN-AIDS estimates that the number of orphaned children in sub-Sahara region will raise to an expected 25,000,000 to 40,000,000 (40 million by the year 2020.Significantly at higher risk of HIV infection, emotionally vulnerable and financially desperate are orphaned children- who are more likely to be sexually abused and forced into exploitive situations- such as prostitution as a means of survival in case of the girl children, as for boy children they become destitutes, sleeping and begging for food on the streets. A lot of children in sub-Sahara suffer from stigmatization, sexual exploitation and inadequate education. Aids prevention instruction, poverty eradication and financial support are sorely needed.

Empowering-families and households in various - communities as to respond effectively to the needs of orphaned and vulnerable children in their care.

Putting in place, effective intervention strategies such as learning programmes, seminars and workshops on HIV /AIDS in order to mitigate the impact of the scourge of HIV / AIDS among families in communities. To work with other NGO’s and ensure support to families for the wellbeing of the orphaned and vulnerable children.
